# Accessibility Features and Design Choices for Autistic Users

## Introduction
This document outlines the various accessibility features and design choices made for the Autism Health Tracker application, focused on enhancing usability for autistic users.

## Visual Accessibility
- **High Contrast Colors:** The app employs a high contrast color scheme to ensure readability.
- **Customizable Fonts:** Users can adjust font sizes and types to their preference, providing options for easier reading.

## Cognitive Accessibility
- **Simple Language:** Text throughout the app uses straightforward language, avoiding jargon to enhance comprehension.
- **Consistent Navigation:** The navigation structure is consistent and predictable, reducing cognitive load and confusion.

## Sensory Considerations
- **Audio Options:** Users can opt-in to auditory feedback or notifications, which can be useful for engagement without overwhelming sensory input.
- **Vibration Feedback:** The app provides tactile feedback for commands and notifications, catering to users who benefit from sensory input.

## User Control
- **Customizable Features:** Users can modify settings to personalize their experience, such as hiding features they may find distracting.
- **Focus Modes:** The app includes focus modes that minimize distractions and allow users to concentrate on tasks.

## Support Resources
- **Help Section:** A comprehensive help section is available for users seeking further assistance with the app.
- **Feedback Channels:** Users are encouraged to provide feedback regarding accessibility features, which will be used to improve future iterations of the application.

## Conclusion
The Autism Health Tracker is committed to providing an inclusive and accessible experience for autistic users. Continuous feedback and updates will ensure that our application evolves to meet the needs of all users effectively.
